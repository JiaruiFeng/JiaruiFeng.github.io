In VAE, we have intractable density function with latent $z$. What if we give up on explicitly modeling density, and just want ability to sample? Thus we get the Generative Adversarial Network(GAN). GAN is a framework for teaching a DL model to capture the training data's distribution so we can generate new data from that same distribution. 

### 1. Architecture of GAN

Typically, GAN have two components, one is **Generator**, the second is **Discriminator**. Given a randomly noise input, the function of Generator is to generate "fake" data that look like the training data. The job of the discriminator is to look at data  and output whether or not it is a real data or a fake data generated by the generator：

<img src="/assets/images/image-20200625234527270.png" alt="image-20200625234527270" style="zoom:50%;" />

During training, the generator try to generate fake data that can cheat the discriminator, while the discriminator is working to become a better detective and correctly classify the real and fake data . Ideally, if the generator can generate data that exact have some distribution with real data, the discriminator get the accuracy about 50%.

Let $D_{\theta_d}(x)$ be the probaility that discriminator predict $x$ as real data instead of fake data, adn $G_{\theta_g}(z)$ be the fake data generate by generator with random input $z$. The goal of $G_{\theta_g}$ is to estimate the distribution that the training data comes from ($p_{data}$) so it can generate fake samples from that estimated distribution ($p_g$). Thus, $D_{\theta_d}(G_{\theta_g}(z))$ is the probability that generator $G_{\theta_g}$ generate fake data that discriminator regard as real data. Thus, the objective function in GAN is a minmax game:


$$
\min _{\theta_{g}} \max _{\theta_{d}}\left[\mathbb{E}_{x \sim p_{\text {data}}} \log D_{\theta_{d}}(x)+\mathbb{E}_{z \sim p(z)} \log \left(1-D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}(z)\right)\right)\right]
$$


Where $D_{\theta_d}$ tries to maximize the probability it correctly classifies reals and fakes ($\log D_{\theta_{d}}$ ), and $G_{\theta_g}$ tries to minimize the probability that $D_{\theta_d}$ predict its output as fake.

During training, we can optimize GAN by alternating optimize discriminator and generator:

* Gradient ascent on discriminator:
  
  
  $$
\max _{\theta_{d}}\left[\mathbb{E}_{x \sim p_{\text {data}}} \log D_{\theta_{d}}(x)+\mathbb{E}_{z \sim p(z)} \log \left(1-D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}(z)\right)\right)\right]
  $$
  
  
* Gradient descent on generator:
  
  
  $$
  \min _{\theta_{g}} \mathbb{E}_{z \sim p(z)} \log \left(1-D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}(z)\right)\right)
  $$



However, in practical, we find that optimizing generator by this not work well. The reason is behind the gradient of the objective function $\log \left(1-D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}(z)\right)\right)$:

<img src="/assets/images/image-20200626174421209.png" alt="image-20200626174421209" style="zoom:50%;" />

We can find that the gradient of function is flat when the sample is likely to be fake, which make hard to update in the beginning. To fix it, we instead do gradient ascent on generator with different objective:


$$
\max _{\theta_{g}} \mathbb{E}_{z \sim p(z)} \log \left(D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}(z)\right)\right)
$$


This time, function wil have higher gradient signal for bad samples.

### 2. Training Algorithm of GAN

The complete training algorithm of GAN is follow:

* **For** number of training iterations **do**

  * **For** k **do**

    * Sample minibatch of $m$ noise samples $\{z^{(1)},...,z^{(m)}\}$ from noise prior $p_g(z)$.

    * Sampleminibatch of $m$ examples $\{x^{(1)},...,x^{(m)}\}$ from data generating distribution $p_{data}(x)$.

    * Update the discriminator by ascending its gradient:
      
      
      $$
    \nabla_{\theta_{d}} \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m}\left[\log D_{\theta_{d}}\left(x^{(i)}\right)+\log \left(1-D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}\left(z^{(i)}\right)\right)\right)\right]
      $$

    

    **End for**
  
  * Sample minibatch of $m$ noise samples $\{z^{(1)},...,z^{(m)}\}$ from noise prior $p_g(z)$.
  
* Update the generator by ascending its gradient:
    
    
    $$
    \nabla_{\theta_{g}} \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m} \log \left(D_{\theta_{d}}\left(G_{\theta_{g}}\left(z^{(i)}\right)\right)\right)
    $$
  
  
  
  **End for**

However, the training of GAN is still tricky, it is easy for GAN to collapse during training. There are many parctical tricks to help trianing GAN and this still an active study field.
