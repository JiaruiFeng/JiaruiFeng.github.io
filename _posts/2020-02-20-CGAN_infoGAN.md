After GAN be put forward, many researchers see the power of GAN in generating data. However, Naive GAN cannot control what kinds of data it generated. To improve the GAN, several structure have been put forward. 

### 1.Conditional Generative Adversarial Network(CGAN)

To achieve more control of what data GAN generated, one idea is to add some additional information to let GAN focus on specific type of data. This can be achieved by conditional model, which result in conditional generative adversarial network. Let the extra information be $y$, $y$ can be any kind of auxiliary information, such as class labels or data from other modalities. We can perform the conditioning by feeding $y$ into the both the discriminator and generator as additional input layer. The structure of CGAN is follow:

<img src="/assets/images/image-20200627183600557.png" alt="image-20200627183600557" style="zoom:50%;" />

In generator, we combine the prior input noise $p_z(z)$ and extra information $y$. After generator generate new data based on both $z$ and $y$, the data is inputed to discriminator with same extra information $y$. The work of discriminator is still predict whether the input is real or fake data. The objective function of CGAN is:


$$
\min _{G} \max _{D} V(D, G)=\mathbb{E}_{x \sim p_{\text {data }}(\boldsymbol{x})}[\log D(x \mid y)]+\mathbb{E}_{z \sim p_{z}(z)}[\log (1-D(G(z \mid y)))
$$


In partical, the $y$ is typically a one-hot vector if $y$ indicate the class of data.

### 2. InfoGAN

In standard GAN, generator uses a simple fatcored continuous input noise vector $z$, while imposing no restrictions on the manner in which the generator may use this noise. As a result, it is possible that the noise will be used by the generator in a highly entangled way, casuing the individual dimensions of $z$ to not correspond to semantic features of the data.

In InfoGAN, model introduce latent code $c$, which target the salient structured semantic features of the data distribution to control the generation. For example, in MNIST dataset, image have specific label to indicate value, meanwhile, the digit's angle and thickness of the digit's stroke also can help during generating. Thus, in generator of InfoGAN, we have two inputs, the first is noise vector $z$, the second is latent code $c$. This time, generator become $G(z,c)$. However, in standard GAN, the generator is free to ignore the additional latent code $c$ by finding a solution satisfying $p_g(x\lvert c)=p_g(x)$. To solve the problem, infoGAN modify the objective function by using mutual information.

#### Mutual Information

In information theory, mutual information $I(X,Y)$ between $X$ and $Y$,  measures the "amount of information" learned from knowledge of random variable $Y$ about the random variable $X$. For continuous random variable, the formula of mutual information is:


$$
I(X,Y)=\int_x \int_yp(x,y)\log \frac{p(x,y)}{p(x)p(y)}dxdy
$$


Or, we can write it as entropy form:


$$
I(X,Y)=H(X)-H(X|Y)=H(Y)-H(Y|X)
$$


When $X$ and $Y$ are independent, then $I(X,Y)=0$, because knowing one variable reveals nothing about the other. Instead, if $X$ and $Y$ are related by a deterministic invertible function, we have maximal mutual information. 

#### Objective of InfoGAN

Now, the objective of InfoGAN become: given any $x$ generated by $p_g(x)$, we want $p_g(c\lvert x)$ to have small entropy, in other word, let the mutual information between $c$ and $G(z,c)$ be maximized:


$$
\min _{G} \max _{D} V_{I}(D, G)=V(D, G)-\lambda I(c , G(z, c))
$$


Where $V(D,G)$ is objective function in standard GAN.

Now, let dig into the computation of $I(X,Y)$. We have


$$
\begin{aligned}
I(X,Y)&=H(X)-H(X|Y)\\
&=H(X)- \int p(y)H(X|Y=y)dy\\
&=H(X)-E_{y}H(X|Y=y)\\
&=H(X)-E_y \int -p(x|y)\log p(x|y)dx\\
&=H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log p(x|y)
\end{aligned}
$$


Now, we look at $I(c , G(z, c))$:


$$
\begin{aligned}
I(c,G(z,c))&=H(c)+E_{x\sim G(z,c)}[E_{c^{\prime} \sim p(c|x)}[\log p(c^{\prime}|x)]]
\end{aligned}
$$


Where $c^{\prime}$ refer to conditional distribution $p(c\lvert x)$. However, in GAN, it is hard to compute $p(c \lvert x)$ directly. Remeber in VAE we use variational inference to avoid computation of conditional distribution. Similarly, we can apply it here:


$$
\begin{aligned}
I(X,Y)&=H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log p(x|y)\\
&=H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log \frac{p(x|y)q(x|y)}{q(x|y)}\\
&=H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log q(x|y)+E_y E_{x|y}\log \frac{p(x|y)}{q(x|y)}\\
&=H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log q(x|y)+E_y D_{KL}(p(x|y)||q(x|y))
\end{aligned}
$$


We know that $D_{KL}(p(x\lvert y)\lvert\rvert q(x\lvert y))\geq0$, the lower bound of $I(X,Y)$ is :


$$
I(X,Y)\geq H(X)+E_y E_{x|y}\log q(x|y)
$$


We can use $q(x\lvert y)$ to approximate $p(x\lvert y)$ using variational inference. Back to InfoGAN, we define an auxiliary distribution $Q(c\lvert x)$:


$$
\begin{aligned}
I(c,G(z,c))&=H(c)+E_{x\sim G(z,c)}[E_{c^{\prime} \sim p(c|x)}[\log p(c^{\prime}|x)]]\\
&\geq H(c)+E_{x\sim G(z,c)}[E_{c^{\prime} \sim p(c|x)}[\log Q(c^{\prime}|x)]]
\end{aligned}
$$


Finally, we can define $L_I(G,Q)$ as:


$$
\begin{aligned}
L_I(G,Q)&=E_{c\sim p(c), x\sim G(z,c)}[\log Q(c|x)]+H(c)\\
&=E_{x\sim G(z,c)}[E_{c^{\prime}\sim p(c|x)}[\log Q(c^{\prime}|x)]]+H(c)\\
&\leq I(c, G(z,c))
\end{aligned}
$$


The objective function of InfoGAN is:


$$
\min _{G, Q} \max _{D} V_{\mathrm{InfoGAN}}(D, G, Q)=V(D, G)-\lambda L_{I}(G, Q)
$$



#### Implementation of InfoGAN

Now, we have the objective function of InfoGAN. To implement it, InfoGAN have two discriminators, the first discriminator $D_1$ is as same as standard GAN that output classification result whether the data is real or fake. The second discriminator $D_2$ is used to parametrize the auxiliary distribution $Q$, the output of this discriminator is predicted $c$ based on input $x$. To reduce computation, two discriminators will share the weights except the last layer.

There are basically two kinds of latent code, namely categorical and continuous latent code. For the categorical latent code, we can simply convert it to one hot vector, in the $D_2$, the output of categorical latent code is just one-hot with same one-hot. We can use cross entropy to chracaterize the difference between $Q(c\lvert x)$ and $c$. For continuous latent code, InfoGAN treat it as a factored Gaussian. In generator, the input of $c$ is continuous value, and the ouput of $D_2$ is mean and variance of predicted $Q(c\lvert x)$. We know that for Gaussian:


$$
p(x)=\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}
$$


Thus the $E_{c\sim p(c), x\sim G(z,c)}[\log Q(c\lvert x)]$ is just $log\ p(c)$:


$$
-\frac{(c-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}-\log(\sigma\sqrt{2\pi})
$$


Let mean and standard deviation of $D_2$ be $Q(x)_{\mu}$ and $Q(x)_{\sigma}$, then:


$$
L_I(G,Q)=-\frac{(c-Q(x)_{\mu})^2}{2Q(x)^2_{\sigma}}-\log (Q(x)_{\sigma}\sqrt{2\pi})+H(c)
$$
